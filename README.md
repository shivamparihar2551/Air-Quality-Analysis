This study demonstrates the application of a Linear Regression model to explore the relationship between latitude and pollution levels. The results indicate a weak but slightly positive trend, suggesting that pollution may increase marginally with higher latitude. However, the low correlation highlights that latitude alone is not a reliable predictor of air quality. Pollution is influenced by multiple factors such as industrialization, traffic, urbanization, and climate. Therefore, incorporating additional environmental and pollutant-related variables is essential for improving model accuracy. Overall, the model provides useful insights and establishes a foundation for more advanced, data-driven air quality analysis in future research.
